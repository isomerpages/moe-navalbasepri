<h3><strong>Launch of Go Green SG 2026</strong></h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>On May 11, Naval Base Primary School (NBPS) took center stage as the host
for the official launch of <strong>Go Green SG 2026</strong>. We were deeply
honored to welcome Deputy Prime Minister Gan Kim Yong, alongside Minister
for Sustainability and the Environment Grace Fu and Senior Parliamentary
Secretary Goh Hanyan, to our campus to kickstart this nationwide movement.</p>
<p><strong>A Garden of Resilience</strong> 
</p>
<p>The highlight of the event was the unveiling of our refreshed <strong>Wellness Garden</strong>.
DPM Gan toured the facility, which now features a cutting-edge solar-powered
hydroponics system. This garden is more than just a green space; it is
a living classroom where our students learn about food security and renewable
energy firsthand.</p>
<p>Through our "Garden-to-Community" initiative, the produce grown by our
students is distributed via community fridges to support our neighbors,
teaching Navalites the importance of social responsibility and sustainability.</p>
<p><strong>Building a Climate-Ready Future</strong> 
</p>
<p>As part of the 2026 Year of Climate Adaptation, our students will continue
to lead the way—whether it’s through our textile upcycling workshops with
partners like Cloop or managing our e-waste recycling programs.</p>
<p>We are incredibly proud of our students and staff for making this launch
a resounding success and aspire to continue to stay the course and build
a greener, more resilient Singapore together.</p>
